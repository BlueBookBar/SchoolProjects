<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today.27s_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>

<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><a href="/wiki/File:President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg" class="image" title="Andrew Johnson"><img alt="Andrew Johnson" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg/100px-President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg" width="100" height="133" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg/150px-President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg/200px-President_Andrew_Johnson.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1617" data-file-height="2156" /></a></div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson" title="Andrew Johnson">Andrew Johnson</a></b> (1808–1875) was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_the_United_States" title="List of Presidents of the United States">17th</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, serving from 1865 to 1869. Johnson was elected to the House of Representatives in 1843, became <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Tennessee" title="Governor of Tennessee">Governor of Tennessee</a> in 1853, and was elected to the Senate in 1857, where he sought passage of the <a href="/wiki/Homestead_Act" title="Homestead Act" class="mw-redirect">Homestead Bill</a>. As <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">Southern states</a>, including Tennessee, seceded to form the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederacy</a>, Johnson remained firmly with the Union. As a <a href="/wiki/War_Democrat" title="War Democrat" class="mw-redirect">War Democrat</a> in 1864, he was a logical choice as <a href="/wiki/Running_mate" title="Running mate">running mate</a> for President <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>, who wished to send a message of national unity. Johnson was sworn in as vice president in March 1865; six weeks later, the <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Lincoln" title="Assassination of Lincoln" class="mw-redirect">assassination of Lincoln</a> made him president. Johnson directed the seceded states to hold conventions and elections to re-form their civil governments. When Congress passed bills for a more severe <a href="/wiki/Reconstruction_Era" title="Reconstruction Era">Reconstruction</a>, he vetoed them, but Congress overrode him, setting a pattern for the remainder of his presidency. When he tried to dismiss Secretary of War <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Stanton" title="Edwin Stanton" class="mw-redirect">Edwin Stanton</a> in possible violation of the <a href="/wiki/Tenure_of_Office_Act_(1867)" title="Tenure of Office Act (1867)">Tenure of Office Act</a>, he <a href="/wiki/Impeachment_of_Andrew_Johnson" title="Impeachment of Andrew Johnson">was impeached</a> by the House of Representatives, and narrowly avoided removal from office. Although his ranking has fluctuated over time, he is generally considered <a href="/wiki/Historical_rankings_of_Presidents_of_the_United_States" title="Historical rankings of Presidents of the United States">among the worst</a> American presidents. (<a href="/wiki/Andrew_Johnson" title="Andrew Johnson"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1071" title="1071">1071</a> – <a href="/wiki/Italo-Norman" title="Italo-Norman">Norman forces</a>, under the command of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Guiscard" title="Robert Guiscard">Robert Guiscard</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Bari" title="Siege of Bari">conquered</a></b> the city of <a href="/wiki/Bari" title="Bari">Bari</a>, the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Catepanate_of_Italy" title="Catepanate of Italy">Catepanate of Italy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1638" title="1638">1638</a> – A rebellion by <a href="/wiki/Kirishitan" title="Kirishitan" class="mw-redirect">Catholic Japanese</a> peasants in <a href="/wiki/Shimabara,_Nagasaki" title="Shimabara, Nagasaki">Shimabara</a> over increased taxes was <b><a href="/wiki/Shimabara_Rebellion" title="Shimabara Rebellion">put down</a></b> by the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a>, resulting in greater enforcement of the policy of <a href="/wiki/Sakoku" title="Sakoku">national seclusion</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1912" title="1912">1912</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Passenger_ship" title="Passenger ship">passenger liner</a> <b><a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS <i>Titanic</i></a></b> sank <i>(pictured)</i> about two hours and forty minutes after <b><a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_RMS_Titanic" title="Sinking of the RMS Titanic">colliding with an iceberg</a></b>, killing more than 1,500 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1986" title="1986">1986</a> – <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_United_States" title="Military of the United States" class="mw-redirect">U.S. armed forces</a> <b><a href="/wiki/1986_United_States_bombing_of_Libya" title="1986 United States bombing of Libya">began bombing</a></b> Libya to try to reduce that country's ability to support international terrorism.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> – At a <a href="/wiki/General_Agreement_on_Tariffs_and_Trade" title="General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade">GATT</a> ministerial meeting in <a href="/wiki/Marrakesh" title="Marrakesh">Marrakesh</a>, Morocco, representatives of 124 countries and the <a href="/wiki/European_Communities" title="European Communities">European Communities</a> signed <a href="/wiki/Marrakech_Agreement" title="Marrakech Agreement" class="mw-redirect">an agreement</a> to establish the <b><a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Organization" title="World Trade Organization">World Trade Organization</a></b>.</li>
</ul>

<p>The <i><b><a href="/wiki/Sleeping_Venus_(Giorgione)" title="Sleeping Venus (Giorgione)">Sleeping Venus</a></b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Oil_painting" title="Oil painting">oil painting</a> on canvas begun by the Italian <a href="/wiki/The_Renaissance" title="The Renaissance">Renaissance</a> master <a href="/wiki/Giorgione" title="Giorgione">Giorgione</a> and completed by <a href="/wiki/Titian" title="Titian">Titian</a> after the former's death in 1510. One of Giorgione's last works, it portrays a nude woman whose profile seems to follow that of the hills in the background. This lone <a href="/wiki/Nude_(art)" title="Nude (art)">nude</a>, unprecedented in <a href="/wiki/Western_painting" title="Western painting">Western painting</a> at the time, influenced numerous future artists. The work is now held in the <a href="/wiki/Gem%C3%A4ldegalerie_Alte_Meister" title="Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister">Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>, Germany.</p>
